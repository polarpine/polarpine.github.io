    Wireframing

    By Polarpine July 06, 2014 Tweet Like +1

    Wireframing is a great tool to visually tackle a page. Here’s my first go at reverse engineering my favorite sites web design processes.

    The Onion

    The Onion is laid out to mirror a newspaper. The general goal of the site is to provide viewers with sattire, in the form of fake news stories. To appear ‘newsy’ it looks like an old fashioned newspaper. The user needs to be grabbed by headlines in order to proceed further into the site. The bolding of titles and pics accomplishes this. Across the top are links to ‘sections’ mirroring sections in a real paper, further adding the the experience that the reader is viewing real news.

    Wikipedia

    Wikipedia is trying to give you information. As much or as little as you want. It accomplishes this by having a large middle section of text/details with a constant sidebar containing key points. I love the simple layout of this site.

    Bay Area Mountain Bike Rides

    The wireframing on Bay Area Rides looks a little cluttered, which follows with looking at the actual site and seeing it lacks visual appeal. There is a key points sidebar, which I’m learning is something I really like in web design. The goal of this site is to give a user options and information on bike rides in a geographic location, with pertinent details about the rides. This is accomplished.
